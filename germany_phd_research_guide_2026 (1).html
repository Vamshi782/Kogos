Max Planck is Germany's flagship fundamental research organisation — equivalent to the Indian CSIR but 10x better funded. Where to find positions

6 Portals to Find Your PhD Position

academics.com
academics.com
Germany's largest academic job portal. Filter by "Doktorand" or "PhD" and your subject. Updated daily. Most Max Planck and university positions are listed here.

euraxess.ec.europa.eu
EU-wide research job portal. Filter by Germany + PhD + your field. Thousands of funded positions. Official EU portal — very reliable.
Research in Germany
research-in-germany.org
Official German government portal for international researchers. Scholarships, PhD programs, funding — all in one place. Made specifically for Indians and international students.
DAAD Portal
daad.de
Germany's official academic exchange portal. Apply for DAAD scholarships here. Also use the Research Explorer to find PhD programs at German universities by subject.
IMPRS Max Planck

Directory of all International Max Planck Research Schools. Find your field → find the school → apply directly. Most have annual application cycles in October–January.
FindAPhD Germany
findaphd.com/phds/germany
International PhD search engine. Filter by Germany + your subject. Good for finding English-language programs across all German universities and institutes.
After your PhD

🚀 PhD in Germany = Permanent Path to Europe

This is why Germany is the smartest move. Your PhD is not just a degree — it is a 3–4 year visa, a salary, and a permanent residency pathway all in one. After your PhD, you qualify for the EU Blue Card immediately.

→
During PhD (3–4 years): Paid €1,500–€2,800/month. Free healthcare. Build German work experience and network.
→
After PhD — EU Blue Card: With your German PhD + job offer, you get EU Blue Card in 4–8 weeks. Salary ≥ €45,300/yr needed — all research and industry jobs comfortably exceed this.
→
21 months to Permanent Residency: EU Blue Card holders who pass B1 German test can apply for PR in just 21 months. Standard is 5 years.
→
Industry salary after PhD: Research Scientist at Siemens, BASF, Bayer, or Bosch = €65K–€90K/year. Your PhD from Max Planck or Fraunhofer makes you extremely competitive.
→
Fraunhofer PhD bonus: Since Fraunhofer works directly with industry, many PhD students receive job offers from their funding company before they even finish their dissertation.
Your roadmap

How to Apply — Step by Step

1
Identify Your Research Area and 3 Target Institutes
Be specific. Not "chemistry" — "organic synthesis" or "computational chemistry". Go to academics.com or IMPRS directory. Search your subject. Find 3 institutes that match. Read their recent publications.
2
Contact the Professor BEFORE Applying
German academic culture: email the professor whose research matches yours. Send a 5-line email: who you are, what you work on, one specific paper of theirs you read, and ask if they have open positions.
"Dear Prof. [Name], I am [Your Name] from India, MSc in [field]. Your recent work on [specific paper topic] connects closely with my thesis on [your topic]. Do you have any PhD openings?"
3
Prepare Your Research Proposal (5–10 pages)
Most German PhD applications require a research proposal. This is your biggest differentiator. It should cover: background, your proposed research question, methodology, expected outcomes, and why this institute. DAAD scholarships also require this.
4
Apply to IMPRS Programs (October–January deadline)
Most IMPRS programs have annual applications between October and January. Go to imprs.mpg.de → find your field → submit online application. Documents needed: MSc transcripts, CV, research proposal, 2 reference letters, TOEFL/IELTS optional but helps.
Apply to minimum 5–8 IMPRS programs in your field simultaneously
5
Apply for DAAD Scholarship Simultaneously
Go to daad.de → create account → find "Research Grants" for PhD → apply. DAAD India office is in New Delhi. You need a supervisor acceptance letter from a German professor — this is why Step 2 matters. DAAD gives €1,400/month + health insurance + travel.
6
Student Visa → PhD Position → EU Blue Card
Once accepted, apply for a German student or researcher visa from VFS Global in India. Processing time 4–8 weeks. After arrival: register at local Bürgeramt (resident registration). After PhD: apply for EU Blue Card with your job offer.
What you need

Application Document Checklist

✓
MSc degree certificate + transcripts
With English translation if issued in regional language. Minimum 65–70% aggregate for most programs. IMPRS and DAAD prefer 75%+.
✓
CV in European format — 2 pages max
No photo. No DOB. Focus on research experience, publications, lab skills, conferences. Use reverse chronological order.
✓
Research proposal — 5 to 10 pages
Background, research question, methodology, expected outcomes, why this specific institute. This separates accepted candidates from rejected ones.
✓
2 reference letters from professors
Ideally from your MSc supervisor AND one other faculty member who knows your research work well. Should be on official letterhead.
✓
Professor acceptance email (for DAAD)
Email German professors in Step 2 before applying to DAAD. DAAD requires a supervisor letter. Without this, DAAD application cannot proceed.
✓
English proficiency — IELTS 6.5 / TOEFL 90
Many IMPRS programs say "not required" but having an IELTS score strengthens your application significantly. Most Indians already qualify.
✓
Publications or preprints (if any)
Even one publication or conference paper puts you ahead of 90% of applicants. List on CV and research proposal. Preprints on arXiv or bioRxiv count.
Cold email

Professor Email Template — Gets Replies

📧 Email the Professor FIRST — Before Applying Anywhere
Subject: PhD Inquiry — MSc [Your Field] | Interest in Your Work on [Specific Topic] Dear Professor [Last Name], I am [Your Name], an MSc graduate in [Chemistry / Biochemistry / Biotech / CS] from [Your University, India] with a GPA of [X.X] and research experience in [Your specific area]. I read your recent paper "[Exact paper title]" and found your approach to [specific aspect] directly relevant to the work I did during my MSc on [your thesis topic in 1 line]. I am writing to ask whether you have any PhD openings in your group for the upcoming intake. I am applying to the [IMPRS / DAAD / university PhD program] and would be very interested to discuss potential research directions if you have capacity. I have attached my CV and a brief 1-page research summary for your reference. Best regards, [Your Name] | [University, India] | [LinkedIn] | [Google Scholar if available]
💡 Key tip: Always mention a SPECIFIC paper the professor wrote. Professors receive hundreds of generic emails. Mentioning their specific work proves you have done your research and are genuinely interested — this alone doubles your reply rate.
