How to Write Your CV for European Pharma Companies

✓ Always include
  • 1–2 pages maximum. Never more.
  • Start with a 3-line professional summary mentioning your QA/QC specialisation and years
  • Quantify everything — "reduced deviations by 35%", "audited 3 API sites", "approved 120+ batch records"
  • List GMP, GDP, ICH Q10, ISO 9001, pharmacopoeias you have worked with
  • Mention specific instruments and software — HPLC, GC, LIMS, SAP QM
  • List your language skills — English level B2 or above is key
  • Add "Open to relocation — EU Blue Card / HSM Visa eligible" at top
  • Save as PDF named: FirstName_LastName_QA_CV_2026.pdf
✗ Never include
  • Your photo — illegal in some EU countries to ask for
  • Date of birth or age
  • Marital status or family details
  • Religion or caste
  • Salary expectations (unless specifically asked)
  • Objective statements like "seeking a challenging role" — waste of space
  • More than 3 pages
  • Tables and heavy formatting — ATS systems cannot read them

🔗 Your LinkedIn Headline — This Is What Gets You Found

Alternative path — especially for freshers

No European Experience? Come Study First. Then Get Hired.

🎓 The Masters Route — Why It Actually Works Better

If you have 0–2 years of experience and keep getting rejected for European jobs — there is a smarter path. Come to Europe for a 1–2 year Masters degree in Pharmaceutical Sciences, Quality Management, or Regulatory Affairs. By the time you graduate, you have a European degree, European lab experience, and you are already inside the country. Companies treat you as a local candidate. Your chances of getting hired go from 5% to 80%.

1
Apply for a Masters in Pharmaceutical Sciences / Quality Management / Regulatory Affairs at a European university — tuition can be as low as €0–€3,000/year in Germany and Netherlands for public universities
2
During your Masters — do internships and thesis projects at pharma companies. Many become full-time hires from their thesis company alone
3
After graduation — apply with a European degree + EU work authorization. Companies prefer you over candidates applying from India directly
4
Your Indian QA/QC work experience ADDS to your European degree — you become a senior candidate from Day 1. Starting salary jumps to €55K+ immediately
