Before you apply

German CV tips for Indians

German CV ≠ Indian resume

German CVs are called "Lebenslauf." They're typically 1–2 pages, chronological (most recent first), and include a professional photo at the top right. No photo = your CV looks incomplete to German recruiters.

What to include (and what to cut)

  • ✅ Professional photo (formal, white/neutral background)
  • ✅ Date of birth and nationality (standard in Germany, unlike US)
  • ✅ Degree recognition status if applicable
  • ✅ Results-focused bullet points with numbers
  • ✅ Cover letter in German (even A2–B1 level shows effort)
  • ❌ Don't use US resume format — no "objective statement"
  • ❌ Don't list 10+ skills without showing where you used them

Degree recognition

Your Indian degree (B.E., B.Tech, B.Pharm, M.Sc.) must be recognized. Check anabin.kmk.org to see if your university is listed. If not, apply through the official recognition portal at anerkennung-in-deutschland.de. For pharma and food science roles, contact the state-level authority in the city you're applying to.

Outreach

Cold email / LinkedIn templates

Keep it short. Germans value directness and clarity. No fluff. No "I hope this email finds you well." Get to the point in 4–5 lines max.

Template 1 — LinkedIn DM (no open role)
Hi [Name],

I'm a [role] with [X years] experience in [pharma/food science/data].
I noticed [Company] is doing interesting work in [specific area].

I'd love to understand if there's any fit — happy to share my profile.

Best,
[Your name]
Template 2 — Cold email (open role)
Subject: Application — [Role Title] | [Your name]

Hi [Name],

I'm applying for [Role] at [Company]. I have [X years] in [domain],
including [one specific relevant achievement — 1 line].

I'm based in [India/abroad], have a valid degree in [field],
and am open to relocation with visa support.

CV attached. Happy to connect.

[Your name]
[LinkedIn link]
Template 3 — Pharma / Food Science specific
Subject: [Regulatory/QA/Food Science] Profile — [Your name]

Hi [Name],

I'm a [B.Pharm / M.Sc Food Science] graduate with [X years]
at [Company/lab type]. My work covers [QA / regulatory / R&D — be specific].

Interested in opportunities at [Company] where my background
in [specific area] could contribute.

Happy to share my CV. Open to relocation.

[Your name]

⚠️ Honest note on language

Several people in comments will say "you need German for everything in Germany." They're partly right. Outside tech startups and international companies, German is important — especially for pharma, food production, and non-research roles. Learning A1–B1 German before you apply will improve your response rate significantly. It signals commitment and gets you shortlisted faster.
