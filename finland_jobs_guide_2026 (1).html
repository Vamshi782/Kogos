Step by step

How to Get Your Finnish Work Permit

1
Get a Job Offer from a Finnish Employer
This is the only hard requirement. Everything else is paperwork. Apply on company career pages directly. Email the recruiter same day. Mention your work permit eligibility upfront.
Timeline: Your effort — no set time
2
Employer Submits Partial Application
Your Finnish employer fills out their portion of the work permit application through the Enter Finland system (enterfinland.fi). They confirm the role, salary, and working conditions. You do not need to do anything at this stage.
Timeline: 1–3 days (employer side)
3
You Submit Your Part on Enter Finland
Go to enterfinland.fi — create an account — submit your portion of the application. Documents needed: passport, employment contract, educational certificates with English translation, proof of accommodation (letter from employer is fine).
Timeline: Same day — 1 hour max
4
Book Finnish Embassy Appointment in India
Book an appointment at the Finnish Embassy in New Delhi or the consulate in Mumbai. Bring your application confirmation, passport, and all documents. Biometrics will be taken at this appointment.
Timeline: Appointment available within 1–2 weeks
5
Wait for Decision — Finnish Immigration Service (Migri)
Migri processes your application. IT and specialist roles qualify for the Fast Track service — processing in 9–14 business days. Other roles take 1–3 months. You can track your application online.
⚡ Fast Track: 9–14 days | Standard: 1–3 months
6
Collect Permit & Fly to Finland
Once approved, collect your residence permit card from the embassy. Book your flight. Register at your local Finnish population register (DVV) within one week of arrival. Open a Finnish bank account (Nordea or OP Bank for new arrivals).
Timeline: 1 week after permit received

What you need

Documents Checklist

✓
Valid Passport — minimum 12 months validity remaining
Finnish permit is typically issued for 1 year renewable. Your passport must be valid longer than the permit duration.
✓
Employment Contract from Finnish Employer
Must include: role title, salary (must meet minimum threshold), start date, working hours, employer details. In English or Finnish.
✓
Educational Certificates + English Translation
Degree certificate and transcripts. If issued in regional language — get a certified English translation. Notarisation not always required but helps.
✓
CV in Europass or European Format
No photo. No DOB. No marital status. 1–2 pages. Action verbs. Quantified achievements. Save as PDF.
✓
Passport Photos — 2 copies, recent
Standard passport photo size. White background. Taken within last 6 months. Required for biometrics at embassy.
✓
Proof of Accommodation in Finland
Employer letter confirming temporary housing OR a rental agreement. Many Finnish employers provide initial accommodation support.

💡 Tips Specifically for Indians Applying to Finnish Companies

→
Mention Fast Track eligibility upfront. IT roles qualify for 9–14 day processing. Tell the employer this in your first email — it removes their biggest concern about hiring from India.
→
Finnish companies value directness. No long cover letters. 3–4 sentences maximum. What you did, what you built, what you want. Finns respect conciseness over elaborate writing.
→
GitHub is your CV in Finland. Finnish tech companies check GitHub before interviewing. Pin your best 3 projects with clean READMEs. This matters more than your education credentials.
→
Target HCL and Infosys for internal transfer. If you work at HCL or Infosys in India — request a transfer to their Helsinki office. This bypasses the entire job search. Easiest path to Finland.
→
Apply in September–November. Finnish companies plan headcount for next year in Q3/Q4. Applications submitted September to November get the fastest responses for January start dates.
→
Finnish salary negotiation culture. Companies give you their offer — you can ask for 5–10% more once. They will not be offended. They expect one negotiation. Do not accept the first number without asking.
