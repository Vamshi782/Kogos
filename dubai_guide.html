Why Dubai

The Real Reasons Indians Choose Dubai

💰
Zero Tax. Real Money.
A software engineer earning AED 22,000/month in Dubai takes home every single dirham. The same salary in London loses 40% to tax. In Netherlands, 37-49%. Dubai is not just about the number - it is what stays in your pocket.
🇮🇳
Feel at Home Immediately
No other country outside India has this: Hindi accepted everywhere. Indian restaurants, Indian grocery stores, Indian communities in every neighborhood. Bur Dubai and Deira are essentially Indian cities. You will not feel like a foreigner for one day.
🚀
Fast Visa. No Lottery.
Employment visa takes 2-4 weeks once your employer applies. No lottery. No cap. No annual quota. The UAE Green Visa even lets skilled workers sponsor themselves - no employer needed. Self-sponsorship is a game changer.
🏗️
Construction of a New Economy
Dubai is actively building a tech and AI hub. G42 (Abu Dhabi AI giant), Careem, Noon, and dozens of funded startups are all headquartered here. The government is spending $100bn+ on AI and tech infrastructure through 2031.
✈️
4-Hour Flight from India
The only major destination where you can be home for a weekend without feeling jet-lagged. Flights from every Indian metro city: AED 500-1200 return. Most Indian professionals fly home 4-6 times a year.
🌐
Gateway to Africa, Asia, Europe
Dubai is the logistics and financial hub connecting three continents. Regional HQs of Accenture, Amazon, PwC, KPMG, McKinsey are all here - the roles here typically carry regional responsibility across 30+ countries.

Strategic Locations

Dubai's Free Zones — What They Mean for You

Free zones allow 100% foreign ownership and have their own visa rules. If your company is in a free zone, your visa is processed by that zone - faster and more flexible.

Dubai Internet City (DIC)
Tech and IT
Home to Microsoft, Google, Cisco, Oracle, HP, IBM, SAP. The Silicon Valley of the Middle East. Most Indian IT professionals end up here. English-first environment, campus-like setup.
Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC)
Banking and Finance
Operates under English common law - the only place in UAE where this applies. Home to Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, HSBC, Citibank. If you are in finance, this is where you want to be.
Dubai Healthcare City (DHCC)
Healthcare and Pharma
UAE's healthcare hub. Home to 120+ hospitals, clinics, and pharma companies. DHA (Dubai Health Authority) licenses are required - process takes 2-4 months for Indian medical graduates.
Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM)
Fintech and Wealth Management
Abu Dhabi's answer to DIFC. Growing fast. G42 is adjacent. Strong for fintech startups and wealth management professionals. English common law, competitive with DIFC for financial roles.
Step by Step

How to Get Your UAE Work Visa

1
Get a Job Offer from a UAE Employer
Apply directly on company career pages and LinkedIn. Mention your ability to relocate immediately. UAE employers move fast - if they like your profile you typically hear back within a week.
Timeline: Your effort
2
Employer Applies for Your Work Permit
Your employer applies to the Ministry of Human Resources (MOHRE) for an Entry Permit. They need your passport, qualifications, and a job offer letter. Process is online. Most employers have an approved quota already.
Timeline: 1-2 weeks
3
Fly to UAE on Your Entry Permit
Once Entry Permit is approved you fly to Dubai. Within 60 days of arrival you complete the residency stamping process - medical test, Emirates ID registration, and visa stamping on your passport.
Timeline: 2-4 weeks after landing
4
Residence Visa Stamped - You Are Official
With your residence visa you can open a UAE bank account (Emirates NBD, Mashreq, RAKBank all easy for new residents), rent an apartment, and sponsor your family. Your employer covers the visa cost - you pay nothing.
Total timeline: 4-8 weeks end to end

Tips Specifically for Indians Going to Dubai

Negotiate your housing allowance separately. Many UAE employers offer a basic salary plus a housing allowance - ask for AED 3,000-5,000/month housing as a separate line item. This directly impacts your take-home comfort.
Get your documents attested before you leave India. UAE requires degree attestation from HRD Ministry (India) and then UAE embassy. This takes 4-6 weeks. Start this process the moment you get the job offer.
Avoid Deira and Bur Dubai for accommodation if you want a quieter life. JVC, Al Quoz, and Dubai Silicon Oasis offer the best rent-to-quality ratio for Indian professionals.
Save your UAE salary aggressively for the first year. After 2 years, Indian professionals in Dubai typically have savings that would take 8-10 years to accumulate in India at comparable roles.
Know the drawback clearly: no PR path in UAE. After your visa expires you leave. Many Indians treat Dubai as a 3-5 year wealth accumulation phase before Europe, Canada, or returning to India.
